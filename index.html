Get an idea what are you getting into

Computers are continuously changing the way we live, work and play. As the use of computers is becoming more and more prevalent in many fields, development of domain specific software and programming skills are becoming ever more important, quickly turning into the core competency for all kinds of 21st-century workers. However, a growing demand for the technology related jobs in industries and a lack of skilled programmer is pushing Canada towards a major technology talent shortage in recent future. To address this challenge, Thompson Rivers University, upon consultation with local industries, is going to offer short-term coding related training courses. These courses are based on hands-on experiential learning and targeted for general public or professionals from the non-IT field who are looking to change their career to IT fields.
